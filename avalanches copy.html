Hypothermia

What is Hypothermia

Hypothermia is a medical condition that happens when your body loses heat faster than it can produce it, causing your core temperature to drop below 35°C (95°F). This can be life-threatening if not treated quickly.

Symptoms of Hypothermia

Mild Hypothermia: Shivering Cold, pale skin Numbness in fingers/toes Confusion or slight clumsiness Moderate Hypothermia: Intense shivering (then shivering may stop, which is dangerous) Slurred speech Drowsiness or difficulty thinking Slow heart rate and breathing Severe Hypothermia (Medical Emergency): Loss of coordination (stumbling, unable to use hands) Very slow, weak pulse Unconsciousness Can lead to heart failure and death if untreated

Treatment of Hypothermia

Treating hypothermia requires gradually warming the person while preventing further heat loss. Move them to a warm, dry place, remove any wet clothing, and wrap them in dry blankets or warm layers. Offer warm, non-alcoholic beverages if they are conscious, and use body heat or heating packs on the chest, neck, and groin. Move the person to a warm, dry place (out of the cold and wind). Remove wet clothing and replace it with dry layers. Do NOT use direct heat (like heating pads or hot water bottles)—this can cause shock. Call emergency services if severe (unconscious, slow pulse, no shivering).
